"First, anyone who seriously intends to become a philosopher
must "once in his life" withdraw into himself and attempt,
within himself, to overthrow and build anew all the sciences
that, up to then, he has been accepting." ― Edmund Husserl, Cartesian
Meditations: An Introduction to Phenomenology 

Confession: I haven't read much Husserl, or Descartes for that matter. Frankly,
it hurts my brain. But I like
what Husserl says there. I didn't nor do I intend on being a philospher, but I have had this 'once in his life'
experience of overthrowing all the sciences of what I have been accepting my
whole life. 
This epoch was the unraveling of my marriage. Before my divorce, for as long as I can remember, I
had been on a path of Christianity and thus had very conservative political
views. Post divorce, I find myself on the Buddist path of enlightenment, and
having shifted a good ways left on the political scale. How this happened has
been a fascinating experience, and quite accidental too. This series of blog
posts is my attempt to sort out how I accidentally became a Buddist.

When your wife of 14 years quite suddenly and expeditiously ends your marriage
over a reality that is radically different than your own,
you tend to question everything, and become quite solipsistic for a brief
time.  This event rocked me to my core, challenged everything I believed in and
accepted as truth. When your life as you know it is burned to the ground and
all that's left is ashes, beliefs and ideas you had before suddenly look very
different. My undestanding of judgement, suffering, attachment, love, and purpose
in life
were all called into question.
